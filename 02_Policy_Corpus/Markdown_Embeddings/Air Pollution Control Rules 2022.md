**S.R.O. No. 255-Law/2022**—In exercise of the powers conferred by section 20 of the Bangladesh Environment Conservation Act, 1995 (Act No. 1 of 1995), the Government hereby makes the following rules, namely:—

### 1. Short Title and Commencement

(1) These rules may be called the Air Pollution (Control) Rules, 2022.
(2) It shall come into force at once.

### 2. Definitions

(1) In these rules, unless there is anything repugnant in the subject or context,—

* **(a) "Non-Point Source"** means any source of air pollutant emission that does not originate from a single identifiable source or specific point;
* **(b) "Act"** means the Bangladesh Environment Conservation Act, 1995 (Act No. 1 of 1995);
* **(c) "Mobile Source"** means an identifiable air pollutant source that emits while in motion, such as motor vehicles, vessels, trains, aircraft, or any similar source;
* **(d) "National Committee"** means the National Committee on Air Pollution Control constituted under rule 15;

* **(e) "Degraded Air Shed"** means the Degraded Air Shed as defined in clause (g) of sub-section (4) of section 8 of the Brick Making and Brick Kiln Establishment (Control) Act, 2013 (Act No. 59 of 2013);
* **(f) "Schedule"** means any Schedule of these rules;
* **(g) "Section"** means section of the Act;
* **(h) "Point Source"** means any source of air pollutant emission that is uniquely identifiable and originates from a specific place, such as an industrial factory in an industrial estate, construction activities in an under-construction area, etc.;
* **(i) "Emission"** means any air pollutant, gas flow, or accidental discharge transmitted into the atmosphere from any point, non-point, mobile, stationary, indoor, or outdoor source;
* **(j) "Ambient Air Quality"** means the quality of air in any area or region, which is distinct from the quality of air emitted directly from a source of pollution;
* **(k) "Major Air Pollutant"** means the pollutants listed under rule 6;
* **(l) "Air"** means gaseous substances surrounding the earth, mainly a mixture of oxygen and nitrogen;
* **(m) "Air Pollutant"** means any substance present in the air that is harmful or may cause harm to public health, environment, and ecology;
* **(n) "Air Pollution"** means such condition of air where solid, liquid, or gaseous substances are present in the atmosphere in quantities exceeding the prescribed standards, disrupting the normal functions of air (indoor and outdoor), harming public health, plants, and animals, and causing damage to the environment and ecology;
* **(o) "Air Quality Monitoring Station"** means an air quality monitoring station operated or approved by the Department;
* **(p) "Local Government Institution"** means Union Parishad, Upazila Parishad, Zila Parishad, Pourashava, and City Corporation; and
* **(q) "Stationary Source"** means any building or non-mobile structure, facility, or installation from which air pollutants are or may be emitted, such as industrial factories, brick kilns, infrastructure construction work, etc.

(2) Words and expressions used in these rules but not defined herein shall have the same meaning as assigned to them in the Act.

### 3. Determination of Standards for Ambient Air, Odour, etc.

(1) For the purpose of clause (a) of sub-section (2) of section 20 of the Act, the standards of ambient air and odour shall be determined on the basis of the standards specified in Schedule 1 and Schedule 4, respectively.

(2) For the purpose of clause (e) of sub-section (2) of section 20 of the Act, the emission standards for motor vehicles, mechanical vessels, and gaseous emissions from industrial establishments or projects shall be determined on the basis of the standards specified in Schedule 2, Schedule 3, and Schedule 5, respectively.

(3) For the purpose of clause (j) of sub-section (2) of section 20 of the Act, the standard for controlling dust from construction works shall be determined on the basis of the criteria specified in Schedule 6.

### 4. National Air Quality Management Plan

The Director General shall, with the approval of the Government, formulate a time-bound National Air Quality Management Plan incorporating the following matters for the purpose of controlling and mitigating air pollution, namely:—

* **(a)** institutional framework for strategic management and coordination;
* **(b)** effective management of air emitted from mobile, point, or non-point sources and stationary sources including chimneys;
* **(c)** effective management of indoor air quality (Indoor Air Quality);
* **(d)** hazardous pollutant control management;
* **(e)** recognition and promotion of best practices in air quality management;
* **(f)** clean energy (Clean Energy), energy efficient technology (Energy Efficient Technology), and quality of fuel related to gaseous emissions;
* **(g)** air quality monitoring;
* **(h)** data collection and preservation, research, management, and campaigns/publicity regarding air quality;
* **(i)** research on air pollution and assessment of its harmful impacts;
* **(j)** participation and cooperation in the global arena regarding air quality management;
* **(k)** sustainable financial arrangements for air quality management; and
* **(l)** necessary matters for the improvement of air quality and effective prevention and control of pollution.

### 5. Declaration and Management of Degraded Air Shed

(1) If an area becomes a severely polluted area due to the ambient air quality exceeding the standards prescribed under Schedule 1, the Director General may declare such area as a Degraded Air Shed.

(2) For declaring a Degraded Air Shed under sub-rule (1), rare natural, historical, or culturally significant areas shall be considered with higher priority.

---

###### 12740

(3) If any area is declared as a Degraded Air Shed under sub-rule (1), the Director General or any officer authorized by him shall take the following actions, namely:—

* **(a)** formulate a time-bound air quality improvement action plan for the said Degraded Air Shed in consultation with relevant stakeholders;
* **(b)** issue directives to air polluting industrial establishments or projects or any other source of air pollution located in the said Degraded Air Shed to take special preventive measures;
* **(c)** impose necessary controls on the establishment or operation of new industrial establishments, projects, or specific or mobile sources causing air pollution within the said Degraded Air Shed; and
* **(d)** issue directives to relocate or stop the operation of existing industrial establishments, projects, or specific or mobile sources causing air pollution within the said Degraded Air Shed.

(4) The declaration of a Degraded Air Shed under sub-rule (1) may be withdrawn if the air quality in the declared area remains compliant with the ambient air quality standards for 2 (two) consecutive years.

(5) If the declaration of a Degraded Air Shed is withdrawn under sub-rule (4), the action plan formulated for the said Degraded Air Shed shall also be deemed to be cancelled.

### 6. Preparation of List of Air Polluting Activities and Management

(1) For the purpose of these rules, the Director General or any officer authorized by him may publish a list of air polluting industrial activities or projects and air pollution caused by such activities that are harmful to the environment, health, society, economy, ecology, or cultural heritage.

(2) After the publication of the list under sub-rule (1), the Director General or any officer authorized by him shall direct the concerned industrial establishment or project to submit a plan containing specific measures to prevent air pollution.

(3) The Director General or any officer authorized by him shall, after scrutiny, approve the plan submitted under sub-rule (2) with necessary corrections, modifications, additions, or deletions, and direct the concerned industrial establishment or project to implement it properly.

(4) For controlling the listed activities under sub-rule (1), the Director General or any officer authorized by him may impose necessary prohibitions apart from the measures specified in the approved plan under sub-rule (3).

(5) If the concerned industrial establishment or project properly complies with the plan approved under sub-rule (3) and the pollution control measures are effective, the activity of the said industrial establishment or project may be excluded from the list of air polluting activities, and the list may be amended accordingly.

### 7. Air Pollution Prevention Plan

(1) The Director General or any officer authorized by him may declare any air polluting substance or object as a major air pollutant globally or for a specific area, and direct the concerned person or institution to submit a time-bound air pollution prevention plan regarding the said air pollutant.

(2) The Director General or any officer authorized by him shall, after scrutiny, approve the air pollution prevention plan submitted under sub-rule (1) with necessary corrections, modifications, additions, or deletions, and direct the concerned person or institution to implement it properly.

(3) The concerned person or institution shall implement the air pollution prevention plan approved under sub-rule (2) and shall inform the Director General or any officer authorized by him in writing of the progress of implementation every 3 (three) months.

### 8. Air Pollution Control in Industrial Establishments or Projects

(1) If in any industrial establishment, project, or commercial enterprise, due to burning of any substance, operation of industry or project, or combustion of fuel, such gaseous emissions are generated, or are likely to be generated, which exceed or are likely to exceed the gaseous emission standards specified in Schedule 5, the owner, occupier, or person in charge of the said industry, project, or commercial enterprise shall take the following measures for controlling air pollution, namely:—

* **(a)** prevent the emission of harmful air pollutants by adopting the following Best Practicable Means, namely:—
  * **(i)** constructing and operating the plant according to its proper size and design;
  * **(ii)** using appropriate raw materials or fuel;
  * **(iii)** where applicable, proper use of Mist Eliminators, Dust Arrestors, Gas Absorbers, and regulatory equipment;
  * **(iv)** introducing alternative production processes;
  * **(v)** introducing alternative operational processes or methods;
  * **(vi)** keeping proper and adequate data on operational activities; and
  * **(vii)** regular and effective maintenance of the plant;
* **(b)** implement an Air Pollution Control System in accordance with specifications approved by the Director General or any officer authorized by him;

---

###### 12742

* **(c)** submit, along with the application for Environmental Clearance to the concerned office of the Department, a detailed plan, design, and equipment details of the air pollution control system, along with relevant documents, under own signature;
* **(d)** comply with any directives issued by the Director General or any officer authorized by him for the reform, modification, replacement, or installation of additional equipment for the air pollution control system;
* **(e)** keep the air pollution control system continuously operational and properly maintained through Sound Engineering Practice, and ensure that the system remains properly effective;
* **(f)** employ qualified manpower for the continuous and effective operation and maintenance of the air pollution control system, and obtain approval for the manpower structure and their qualifications at the time of receiving the Environmental Clearance; and
* **(g)** arrange necessary equipment for Performance Monitoring of the air pollution control system and regularly monitor the performance of its various parts.

(2) The gaseous emission levels of any industrial establishment or project shall not exceed the standards prescribed under Schedule 5.

(3) The industrial establishment or project shall, at its own expense, periodically monitor the quality of gaseous emissions at a laboratory approved by the Director General and submit the results of the emitted gaseous tests to the Department at specified intervals in accordance with the conditions of the Environmental Clearance.

(4) The results submitted under sub-rule (3) shall be properly preserved by the said industrial establishment or project and shall be displayed during inspection by any officer of the Department.

(5) If deemed expedient considering the environmental sensitivity, the Department may issue directives to the industrial establishment or project and its entrepreneur for Continuous Emission Monitoring, and the concerned industrial establishment or project and its entrepreneur shall arrange for Continuous Emission Monitoring according to the directives and connect it with the Central Air Quality Monitoring Server of the Department.

### 9. Control of Air Pollution from Vehicles

(1) Any person, agency, or institution operating and controlling vehicles shall comply with the standards prescribed in these rules and the work methods recommended in the National Air Quality Management Plan.

---

###### 12743

(2) For the purpose of implementing the standards prescribed in these rules, the Department may advise or make recommendations to the Bangladesh Road Transport Authority (BRTA) or any similar agency to prohibit or restrict the use of polluting vehicles and to introduce environment-friendly vehicles, and BRTA or the similar agency shall take appropriate measures based on such advice or recommendations.

(3) The Bangladesh Road Transport Authority or Bangladesh Road Transport Corporation or any other authority issuing or approving vehicle licenses shall, during the issuance or renewal of licenses or approvals, measure the emission level of the vehicle through its own management or through government-approved institutions, and ensure that it remains within the limits prescribed in Schedule 2 or Schedule 3 of these rules.

### 10. Role of Local Government Institutions and Construction Regulatory Agencies

(1) Local government institutions and construction regulatory agencies shall follow the standards, control methods prescribed in Schedule 6, and the work procedures specified in the National Air Quality Management Plan.

(2) To enable local government institutions to capably perform their duties in air quality management and control in areas under their jurisdiction, the Department may continuously provide technical and training support.

(3) Local government institutions shall manage air quality in areas under their jurisdiction by complying with and enforcing the standards, directives, and restrictions described in the schedules of these rules to control air pollution generated under their authority.

(4) During the execution of any infrastructure construction, reconstruction, renovation, and repair activities operated under local government institutions, the standards prescribed in Schedule 6 shall be complied with to protect the environment.

(5) Local government institutions shall, in accordance with the standards prescribed in Schedule 6, reduce the use of polluting chimneys and ensure the replacement of environment-friendly kilns/furnaces within the time specified by the Director General or any officer authorized by him.

(6) Building construction regulatory agencies shall prevent and control the spreading of dust and sand by complying with the standards prescribed in these rules, and shall effectively monitor construction activities.

(7) According to the Environment Conservation Rules, 1997, plans for the prevention and control of dust and sand must be included at the time of obtaining Environmental Clearance for projects related to infrastructure construction, and its implementation must be ensured.

(8) Uncovered spaces beside roads must be covered with concrete carpeting or grass planting by the concerned authority.

(9) Local government institutions shall undertake tree plantation and afforestation programs in areas under their jurisdiction, and shall set mandatory plans and targets in this regard.

---

###### 12744

(10) To prevent dust pollution, local government institutions shall, through greening, reduce the area of uncovered lands that can generate dust in urban areas and increase per capita greening.

(11) Local government institutions shall ensure greening on roofs and open spaces of all buildings in urban areas and provide necessary directives to the concerned person or institution in this regard.

### 11. Activities of Agencies, Institutions, or Persons Executing Construction, Renovation, and Repair Works

During the execution of construction, reconstruction, repair, or renovation works of roads, drains, buildings, and other infrastructures, the concerned person or institution shall take the following measures for controlling air pollution, namely:—

* **(a)** keep the under-construction building covered, including establishing appropriate temporary canopies or enclosures at the construction site;
* **(b)** keep all types of construction materials (soil, sand, rod, cement, etc.) covered or shielded;
* **(c)** arrange to transport construction materials (soil, sand, cement, brick, waste, etc.) in trucks, vans, or lorries that are covered or completely enclosed;
* **(d)** arrange to operate trucks, vans, or lorries used in transportation and construction works on roads only after completely cleaning mud, soil, or waste from their wheels;
* **(e)** construction materials (soil, sand, cement, etc.) shall not be left on roads, pavements, or anywhere, and waste generated from construction works shall not be stored in the open or burned;
* **(f)** spray water or dust-controlling chemicals at least 2 (two) times a day around the construction, reconstruction, or repair site.

### 12. Control of Air Pollution from Waste

To control air pollution generated from waste, the concerned agency, institution, or person shall take the following measures, namely:—

* **(a)** waste or any part thereof shall not be stored in the open or burned anywhere;
* **(b)** no waste shall be stored in the open or burned beside roads, streets, or highways;
* **(c)** waste extracted from drains, sewers, or ditches shall not be piled up beside roads;
* **(d)** household waste under no circumstances shall be stored in the open or burned in front of houses or beside adjacent streets;

---

###### 12745

* **(e)** to prevent air pollution, residents living in houses shall, on their own responsibility, clean the waste around their houses; and
* **(f)** waste generated in various markets, shopping malls, or commercial establishments shall not be dumped on roads or beside roads, piled up, or burned.

### 13. Air Quality Monitoring and Warning

(1) The Department shall establish and operate an adequate number of Continuous Air Quality Monitoring Stations (Continuous Air Quality Monitoring Station) at appropriate locations across the country for the purpose of observing the state of air quality nationwide.

(2) The Department may establish and operate transboundary air quality monitoring stations at appropriate locations for transboundary air quality monitoring.

(3) All air quality monitoring stations established under sub-rule (1) or (2) shall be connected to the central server of the Department through a Real Time Automation system, and data and information transmitted from the stations shall be preserved.

(4) Through the analysis of data and information preserved under sub-rule (3), the Department shall prepare the daily Air Quality Index (AQI) and publish it through the website of the Department and other media.

(5) If the AQI levels mentioned in sub-rule (4) continuously rise and reach an extremely harmful level from a health perspective, the Department shall issue warning messages to the public through appropriate media and advise the public to take necessary health protection measures.

### 14. Data and Information Management

(1) The Department shall act as the central repository for all data and information related to air quality, and for this purpose, shall operate an information network for the preservation, retrieval, and exchange of data.

(2) The AQI, relevant reports, warnings, and other incidental matters prepared, preserved, and published by the Department shall be accessible to the general public; provided that, for the supply of raw data and information to any agency or individual level, fees shall be paid at the following rates, namely:—

* **(a)** for government agencies, 7 (seven) thousand Taka; and
* **(b)** for other agencies, 12 (twelve) thousand Taka:

Provided that, for use by students, teachers, and researchers of various educational institutions and institutions involved in environmental research, air quality data and information may be provided free of charge with the approval of the Director General based on a written request and recommendation of the head of the agency, educational institution, or department.

### 16. Award

The Government may confer awards on any person or institution for outstanding contributions to the prevention and control of air pollution and the preservation and improvement of air quality.

### 17. Penalty

If any person or institution violates the provisions of clauses (c) and (d) of sub-rule (3) of rule 5, rules 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12, or sub-rule (5) of rule 13 of these rules, such act shall be an offence and for such offence they shall be punished with imprisonment for a term not exceeding 2 (two) years or with fine not exceeding 2 (two) lakh Taka, or with both, in accordance with the provisions of sub-section (2) of section 15 of the Act.

(a) arrangement must be made to wash vehicles using a high-pressure water jet at the vehicle exit path;
(b) the vehicle washing bay and the road connecting the said bay to the vehicle exit path must be paved with concrete, bituminous materials, or hardcore; and
(c) if the construction site is adjacent to any street, road, service lane, or any place accessible to the public, temporary fencing of at least 2.4 meters height must be installed along the entire boundary of the site, except for the entrance and exit paths.

###### 12769

#### 10. Access Road

(a) except for road opening or resurfacing (Road opening or resurfacing) works, main haul roads (Haul road) must be paved with concrete, bituminous materials, hardcore, or metal plates and kept free of dust and sand, or kept wet by spraying water; and
(b) "main haul road (Haul road)" means any path within the construction site through which on average more than 4 vehicles transit every 30 minutes.

#### 11. Cement

(a) stacks of 20 or more cement bags must be completely covered with impervious sheets or kept in a place covered at the top and on three sides;
(b) cement supplied in bulk (bulk cement) must be stored in silos;
(c) cement exceeding the storage capacity of the silo shall not be stored; and
(d) after taking bulk cement or cement out of bags, the cement loading, unloading, transfer, handling, or storage operations must be performed in a completely enclosed system (Enclose system), and an effective fabric filter or any other similar air pollution control device or equipment must be integrated with its vent or exhaust path.

#### Part-4

#### 12. Stockpiling of Dusty Materials

Piles of dusty materials (Stockpile)—
(a) must be completely covered with impervious sheets; or
(b) must be kept in a place covered at the top and on three sides; or
(c) the entire surface must be kept wet by spraying water.

#### 13. Loading, Unloading, and Transfer of Dusty Materials

Before loading, unloading, and transferring of all dusty materials, except for cement and other construction materials where moisture is an issue, water must be sprayed to maintain the dampness of the dusty materials.

#### 14. Transfer of Dusty Materials using a Belt Conveyor System

(a) the top and both sides of the conveyor belt used for transferring dusty materials must be kept covered;
(b) the transfer points between two belts must be kept completely enclosed (Enclosed);

---

###### 12770

(c) an effective belt scraper (Belt scraper) or similar device (Device) must be installed at the head pulley (Head pulley) of the belt conveyor to remove particulate matter adhering to the belt. To prevent material falling from the return belt, a bottom plate (Bottom plate) must be integrated with the belt scraper or a similar measure must be adopted;
(d) arrangement must be made to adjust the level of each stockpiling belt conveyor so that the vertical distance (Vertical distance) between the outlet of the belt conveyor (Outlet) and the material landing point (Material landing point) does not exceed 1 meter; and
(e) the top and three sides of the unloading point of dusty materials from the outlet of the belt conveyor (Outlet) to the stockpile, storage bin, truck, and barge must be kept enclosed.

#### 15. Vehicles used in Construction Works

(a) immediately before leaving the construction site, each vehicle must be washed to remove dusty materials (Dusty materials) from the body and wheels of the vehicle; and
(b) vehicles carrying dusty materials must be covered properly with impervious sheets before leaving the construction site so that particulate matter cannot escape from the vehicle.

#### 16. Drilling, Cutting, and Polishing

Continuous water spraying must be applied, or a dust extraction and filtering device must be installed at the site of any pneumatic or power-operated drilling, cutting, polishing, or mechanical breaking operations.

#### 17. Excavation or Earth Moving

Water must be sprayed to maintain dampness in the area immediately before, during, and after any soil excavation or removal works.

#### 18. Concrete Production

(a) cement supplied in bulk must be stored in silos;
(b) cement exceeding the storage capacity of the silo shall not be stored;
(c) after taking bulk cement or cement out of bags, the cement loading, unloading, transfer, handling, or storage operations must be performed in a completely enclosed system (Enclose system), and an effective fabric filter or any other similar air pollution control device or equipment must be integrated with its vent or exhaust path; and
(d) for producing concrete at any site, the work of taking cement out of bags, batching, and mixing must be carried out in a place covered at the top and on three sides.

###### 12771

#### 19. Site Clearance

(a) water must be sprayed to maintain dampness in the area immediately before, during, and after tree uprooting, clearing of undergrowth, or removal of boulders, poles (Pole), pillars (Pillar), or temporary or permanent structures; and
(b) all demolished materials (Demolished materials) such as uprooted trees, cleared undergrowth, boulders, poles, pillars, structures, debris, waste, and any other materials generated from site clearance that can cause dust must be kept covered with impervious sheets.

**Note:**(1) **"Construction Site"** means a place where construction activities are carried out or where construction materials or equipment are stored (Storage);(2) **"Construction Work"** means:

* **(a)** construction, demolition (demolition), alteration, repair, or maintenance (maintenance) of buildings, bridges, chimneys, docks (dock), arches, hoardings (hoarding), shafts/structures, tunnels, walls, piers (pier), wharves (wharf), roads, slopes, embankments, streets, railways, tramways, airports, channels (channel), drains, service lanes, water supply or drainage ducts, lighting, or public utilities;
* **(b)** dredging;
* **(c)** extraction of any material (Extraction) from land;
* **(d)** piling;
* **(e)** quarrying (Quarrying);
* **(f)** reclamation (Reclamation);
* **(g)** site formation (Site formation);
* **(h)** preparatory activities for executing works specified in (a) to (g); and
* **(i)** use of machinery, plants, tools, gear, and materials for executing activities specified in (a) to (h);

(3) **"Dusty materials"** means cement, soil, aggregates (aggregates), silt, stone (Stone), sand, dust, and wooden chips (wooden chips).
